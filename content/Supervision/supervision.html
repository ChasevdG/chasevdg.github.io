Leveraging a background in mathematical physics and differential geometry, I aim to investigate data embeddings within hyperbolic space during my internship. The focus lies especially on representations of graphs as models for neuron morphologies. Because hyperbolic space naturally 'expands' to accommodate branching structures, it’s the perfect lens for identifying potential hierarchical structures in neuron morphologies.

Sameeksha Sriram

Masters Student Intern

I am interested in how geometric structure can improve the way deep learning models represent scientific data. My current work focuses on Clifford algebraic positional encodings for transformer models, where rotations are formulated using geometric algebra. By extending positional encodings beyond standard vector-based representations, this project aims to help models capture richer spatial relationships in images, videos, spherical data, molecules, and other high-dimensional structured domains.

Roger Lozano Freixanet

Bachelor Thesis Student

My current research in stochastic Itô analysis aims to expand stability results for stochastic differential equations driven by Brownian motion via the Lyapunov method. With this generalization, I aim to provide a rigorous framework for the synchronization of the Kuramoto model perturbed with white noise, a current gap in the literature, and prove exponential convergence towards synchronization, explicitly quantifying the rate of convergence and the size of the random basin of attraction. The Kuramoto model has surged in importance in recent times as a state-of-the-art paradigm for Kuramoto oscillatory artificial neurons and Kuramoto diffusion.
